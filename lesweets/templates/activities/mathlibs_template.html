REVIEW

ADJECTIVE describes something or somebody. Short, soft, funny, and stinky are adjectives.

ADVERB tells how something is done. It modifies a verb and usually ends in "ly." Modestly, greedily, quickly, and carefully are adverbs.

EXCLAMATION is a sudden cry or remark to express how you are feeling. Ouch! Hey! and Oops! are all exclamations.

NOUN is the name of a person, place, or thing. Teacher, school, nose, and dog are nouns.

PLURAL NOUN means more than one of a person, place, or thing. For example, cats pluralized is cats

VERB is an action word. Run, jump, walk, and sit are verbs. Put the verbs in past tense if the directions say PAST TENSE. Ran, jumped, walked, and sat are verbs in the past tense.
